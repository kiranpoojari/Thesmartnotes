Introduction to Spectroscopy - The Smart Notes

Spectroscopy is the study of the interaction between matter and electromagnetic radiation. It plays a crucial role in scientific research, industrial applications, and analytical chemistry. Spectroscopy helps identify chemical structures, determine molecular compositions, and analyze unknown substances.

Why Study Spectroscopy?

  • ✅ Essential in Analytical Chemistry – Helps identify molecular structures and chemical compositions.
  • ✅ Widely Used in Research – Applied in physics, chemistry, biology, and medicine.
  • ✅ Industrial Applications – Used in pharmaceuticals, food science, and material testing.
  • ✅ Non-Destructive Analysis – Allows sample analysis without altering its structure.

Who Can Benefit?

  • 🎓 Chemistry and Physics students studying molecular analysis.
  • 🔬 Researchers in pharmaceuticals and material science.
  • 🏭 Professionals in industrial quality control and forensic science.

What is Spectroscopy?

Spectroscopy is a scientific technique that analyzes how light interacts with matter. Different types of spectroscopy provide insights into molecular structures, functional groups, and atomic composition.

Types of Spectroscopy:

  • 🔹 UV-Visible Spectroscopy – Measures light absorption in the ultraviolet and visible range.
  • 🔹 Infrared (IR) Spectroscopy – Identifies functional groups in organic molecules.
  • 🔹 Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) – Analyzes molecular structure using magnetic fields.
  • 🔹 Mass Spectrometry (MS) – Determines molecular weight and composition.
  • 🔹 Raman Spectroscopy – Used in material science and biological studies.

Spectroscopy plays a crucial role in scientific advancements, enabling researchers to study substances at atomic and molecular levels.
