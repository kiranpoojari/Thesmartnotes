Introduction to DC Motor

A **DC Motor (Direct Current Motor)** is an electrical machine that converts electrical energy into mechanical motion. It operates based on the principle of **electromagnetic induction**, where a magnetic field interacts with a current-carrying conductor to produce motion. DC motors are widely used in industrial automation, robotics, automotive applications, and home appliances due to their efficiency and speed control.

Types of DC Motors

  • Brushed DC Motor: Uses brushes and a commutator for current direction, commonly found in low-cost applications.
  • Brushless DC Motor (BLDC): More efficient, longer lifespan, and widely used in electric vehicles and drones.
  • Shunt DC Motor: Has parallel field windings, providing a constant speed.
  • Series DC Motor: Offers high torque but has speed variations.
  • Compound DC Motor: A combination of shunt and series motors for balanced performance.

Applications of DC Motors

DC Motors are widely used in various industries and everyday devices:

  • Electric Vehicles (EVs): Provides torque and efficient power transmission.
  • Industrial Machinery: Used in conveyor belts, pumps, and fans.
  • Robotics & Automation: Enables precise motor control in robotic arms.
  • Home Appliances: Found in washing machines, mixers, and vacuum cleaners.
  • Medical Equipment: Used in prosthetic limbs and ventilators.
